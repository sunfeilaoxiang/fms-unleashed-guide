    • Introduction

    ▼

    Bienvenue dans le guide de gestion des stocks Unleashed de Feel My Skin. Ce guide est votre référence complète pour gérer les stocks, les clients, les fournisseurs, les commandes et les rapports dans Unleashed.

    Qu'est-ce que Unleashed ?

    Unleashed est un système de gestion des stocks basé sur le cloud qui suit les stocks dans plusieurs entrepôts, gère les commandes de vente et d'achat, et fournit des rapports et analyses détaillés. Pour Feel My Skin, il s'intègre avec Shopify (e-commerce), les systèmes de point de vente (magasins de détail) et les systèmes comptables.

    Configuration actuelle de Feel My Skin

    • Entrepôts: DEPOT_FEEL (main), CLICHY (store), GAITE (store), CHATELET (store), plus Romanian locations
    • Sales Channels: Shopify (e-commerce), API/POS (physical stores), Wholesale
    • Produits: ~1,000+ SKUs (Korean skincare products)
    • Integrations: Shopify Hub for e-commerce sync, custom API scripts for POS/wholesale orders

    Terminologie clé

    • SKU (numéro de gestion des stocks): Unique product code (e.g., SERUM-001)
    • Bon de commande (BC): Order placed with a supplier to buy inventory
    • Bon de commande client (BC): Order from a customer (Shopify, POS, wholesale)
    • Stock Ajustement: Manual correction to inventory for damage, shrinkage, or count discrepancies
    • Entrepôt Transfert: Movement of stock between locations
    • Sales Group: Channel or source for reporting (Shopify, API, Wholesale)
    ✓

    • Top-performing products and categories

    Every morning, spend 5 minutes on the dashboard to:

    1. Check for low stock alerts – identify products that need reordering
    2. Review pending sales orders – see which orders are waiting to be shipped
    3. Check received shipments – confirm supplier deliveries have arrived

    Gestion des produits

    ▼

    Produits in Unleashed include all items sold by Feel My Skin: serums, creams, sets, etc. Each product has multiple tabs containing pricing, supplier info, cost data, and inventory tracking. FMS uses the FMSK_ prefix for all product codes (e.g., FMSK_11691).

    Creating a New Product (Shopify-First Workflow)

    At FMS, new products are created on Shopify first, then registered in Unleashed:

    1. Create the product on Shopify first — add name, images, price
    2. Shopify generates a product code — copy this code
    3. Go to Inventory → Products → Add Product in Unleashed
    4. Enter the Shopify-generated code as the product code (FMSK_XXXXX format)
    5. Enter the product description
    6. The barcode can be added later — once the product physically arrives at the depot, scan it and add the barcode to the product record
    7. By default, products are set as sellable with diminishing type
    8. Save the product
    ⚠
    Important : Créez toujours le produit sur Shopify en premier pour obtenir le code. Ne créez pas les produits dans Unleashed en premier - cela cause une incompatibilité des SKU avec la synchronisation Shopify.

    Product Record Tabs

    The Details Tab

    Core product information:

    • Product Code: Unique identifier (FMSK_XXXXX)
    • Description: Product name
    • Product Group: Category by brand (e.g., ISNTREE, COSRX)
    • Units of Measure: Standard unit (bottle, jar, etc.)
    • Default Purchase Price: Standard cost from supplier
    • Default Sell Price: Standard retail price
    • Default Supplier: Link each product to its primary supplier
    Product details — Details tab

    The Stocks Tab

    Shows stock levels across all 7 warehouses:

    • Stock on Hand (SOH): Total quantity currently in inventory
    • Available Quantity: SOH minus allocated stock (reserved for pending orders)
    • "On Purchase": Incoming stock from open purchase orders
    ✓
    Conseil : Always check stock across ALL 7 warehouses before reordering to avoid over-purchasing. A product may show zero in DEPOT_FEEL but have stock sitting in CLICHY or TRADE_IASI.

    The Costs Tab

    Tracks product pricing and inventory valuation:

    • Average Landed Cost (ALC): Weighted average cost of your stock — recalculates automatically every time you receipt a new PO
    • Last Cost: The price paid in the most recent purchase order
    🔴
    CRITICAL: Cost Distribution Setting
    Cost distribution must be set to "By Base Quantity", NOT "By Value". This was confirmed by Charlie Fleet during implementation. Using "By Value" causes incorrect ALC calculations, inflated COGS, and distorted margins.

    The Transactions Tab

    Displays all historical movements for a product:

    • All warehouse transfers
    • All sales shipments
    • All purchase receipts
    • All stock adjustments

    You can filter by warehouse, date range, and transaction type. Use this tab to investigate stock discrepancies — it's your audit trail.

    The Purchase Tab & Sale Tab

    Purchase Tab:

    • Default supplier for this product
    • Supplier product code (the SKU assigned by the supplier)
    • Standard purchase price

    Sale Tab:

    • Default sell price
    • Sell price tiers (volume discounts)
    • FMS may have different prices for boutique vs. Shopify e-commerce channels

    Batch Operations

    Batch Changing Product Codes

    To change multiple product SKUs at once (e.g., syncing with Shopify IDs):

    1. Go to Produits menu
    2. Click Change Codes (in the toolbar or Stocks section)
    3. Select Batch to import a template
    4. Upload your CSV with old code, new code, and product names
    5. Verify and click Import

    This is much faster than changing codes one by one and is essential for syncing Shopify SKUs into Unleashed.

    ⚠
    Avertissement : Only modify the SKU or product code if you are certain. Changing codes affects all historical orders and Shopify sync. Use batch changes rather than individual edits to maintain consistency.

    Creating a Customer

    1. Menu → Clients → + New Customer
    2. Enter Customer Code (e.g., "SHOPIFY", "GAITE-POS", "ACME-WHOLESALE")
    3. Enter Nom du client (e.g., "Shopify E-Commerce", "Gaite Store", "ACME Wholesale")
    4. Fill in contact details (email, phone, address) if applicable
    5. Set default currency and payment terms
    6. Click Save

    Importing Clients in Bulk

    If you have a spreadsheet of customers, use the import feature:

    1. Go to Import/Export → Clients
    2. Download the template (CSV)
    3. Fill in: Customer Code, Nom du client, Email, Phone, Address, City, Country
    4. Upload the file back into Unleashed

    Supplier Setup

    Supplier Codes Strategy

    Create one record per supplier company. Include standard information:

    • Supplier Code: Unique identifier (e.g., "AMOREPACIFIC", "ETUDE")
    • Nom du fournisseur: Full company name
    • Contact Details: Email, phone, address
    • Default Entrepôt: Where POs from this supplier typically arrive (e.g., DEPOT_FEEL)
    • Lead Time: Number of days from order date to expected delivery
    • Default Notes: Payment terms, order minimums, contact person, etc.

    Creating a Supplier

    1. Menu → Fournisseurs → + New Supplier
    2. Enter Supplier Code and Nom du fournisseur
    3. Set Default Entrepôt (where stock typically arrives)
    4. Enter Lead Time in days (e.g., 10 days for Korean suppliers)
    5. Add contact details and any default notes
    6. Click Save

    Why Lead Times Matter

    When you create a Purchase Order with a supplier that has a lead time set, Unleashed automatically calculates the expected delivery date. For example:

    • Order Date: January 1
    • Lead Time: 10 days
    • Expected Delivery: January 11

    This helps you plan stock arrivals and avoid stockouts. Always verify and update lead times as supplier relationships change.

    ✓
    Conseil : Set default warehouses on supplier records so that POs automatically route stock to the correct location. This saves time on every order.

    Configuration des prix

    ▼

    Unleashed supports a flexible pricing hierarchy. Different customers and channels can have different prices for the same product.

    Pricing Hierarchy (Top to Bottom)

    Unleashed applies prices in this order. The first match wins:

    1. Customer-Specific Pricing – Custom price for this exact customer
    2. Sale Price Tier – Based on quantity ordered (e.g., buy 10+ units = discount)
    3. Default Sale Price – The default price on the product record

    Setting Customer-Specific Pricing

    For wholesale customers with negotiated prices:

    1. Go to Clients and select the customer
    2. Click on the Customer Pricing section
    3. Add a line with:
      • Product or Product Group (e.g., "SERUM-001" or "Serums")
      • Price Type – "Fixed Price" (e.g., £10) or "Discount Percentage" (e.g., 20%)
      • Valid From / Valid To – Optional date range
    4. Click Save

    Bulk Importing Customer Pricing

    To set prices for many products at once:

    1. Go to Import/Export → Customer Pricing
    2. Download the template
    3. Fill in:
      • Customer Code (e.g., "ACME-WHOLESALE")
      • Product Group (optional – applies to all products in the group)
      • Price/Discount (e.g., "50" for fixed price or "20%" for discount)
      • Valid From / Valid To (e.g., "2026-01-01" to "2026-06-30")
    4. Upload and confirm

    Managing Price Reviews

    For wholesale customers, it's common to review prices quarterly or annually. Use the "Valid To" date to:

    • Set expiry dates for negotiated prices
    • Create automatic reminders to renew pricing agreements
    • Track when prices go back to default after the "Valid To" date

    To extend pricing, update the "Valid To" date and re-save.

    ⚠
    Important : Use Product Groups for bulk pricing changes. If you apply pricing to a product group, ALL products in that group automatically inherit the pricing. This saves time but requires careful planning.

    Intégration Shopify

    ▼

    The Shopify Hub is Unleashed's connection to your e-commerce store. It automatically syncs products and imports sales orders from Shopify.

    How Intégration Shopify Works

    Step 1: Setting Up the Shopify Hub

    The Shopify Hub must be installed and configured before orders can sync:

    1. Menu → Integrations → Shopify
    2. Click Configure
    3. Log in to your Shopify account and authorize the Unleashed app
    4. Configure settings (see below)

    Step 2: Key Configuration Settings

    When configuring the Shopify Hub, pay attention to these critical settings:

    • Default Entrepôt for Shopify Commandes: Set to "Shopify" or your e-commerce fulfillment location
    • Create Missing Produits: Enable this so new Shopify products automatically create in Unleashed (on first order)
    • Create Missing Clients: Typically enabled – creates "Shopify" as the customer
    • SKU Matching: Ensure Shopify SKUs match Unleashed product codes (see SKU Sync below)
    • Sales Group Assignment: Set to "Shopify" for reporting/analytics

    Product SKU Matching (Critical!)

    The SKU Sync Challenge

    For Shopify orders to match to the correct products in Unleashed, the Shopify product SKU must exactly match the Unleashed product code. This requires coordination:

    • Shopify generates product IDs (SKUs) – these may be internal numbers
    • Unleashed product codes are your internal inventory codes (e.g., "SERUM-001")
    • If they don't match, orders come through with no product assigned

    Solution: Batch SKU Sync

    To align Shopify and Unleashed SKUs:

    1. Export your product catalog from Shopify (via Shopify admin)
    2. In Unleashed, go to Produits → Change Codes → Batch
    3. Upload a CSV with:
      • Current Product Code (your Unleashed code)
      • New Product Code (from Shopify SKU)
    4. Verify the changes and import

    After this, new Shopify orders will correctly match to Unleashed products.

    Tax Rate Configuration (CRITICAL for Shopify Sync)

    Understanding the Tax Issue

    Shopify orders include tax rates (20%, 5.5%, etc.), but Unleashed may not recognize these tax rates if they're not configured. When orders sync, Unleashed looks for matching tax rates. If none exist, the order fails to import.

    Setting Up Tax Rates

    For Feel My Skin, you must configure these tax rates:

    1. Menu → Settings → Taxes
    2. Click + New Tax Rate for each rate you need
    3. Add 20% – for standard VAT in France
    4. Add 5.5% – for reduced VAT (or whatever Shopify uses)
    5. Keep the system default at 0% (no tax) for POS/wholesale orders
    6. For each rate, enter a Tax Code (e.g., "20% Sales" or "5.5% Reduced")
    7. Tick Sales Tax checkbox for each
    8. Click Save
    🔴
    CRITICAL: Tax Rate Setup
    NEVER remove the 0% tax rate (the system default). Keep it in place even after adding 20% and 5.5%. The 0% rate is essential for POS/wholesale orders which don't carry tax in Unleashed. Shopify orders use 20%/5.5%, while POS orders use 0%.

    Shopify Order Flow

    How Commandes Appear in Unleashed

    When a customer buys from your Shopify store:

    1. Order appears in Shopify with status "Pending" (not yet fulfilled)
    2. Unleashed receives the order and creates a Sales Order with status "Parked" or "Placed"
    3. When you fulfill the order in Shopify, it updates in Unleashed to "Shipped" or "Completed"

    Fulfillment Workflow

    There are two ways to fulfill Shopify orders:

    • Fulfill in Shopify: Mark items as "Fulfilled" in Shopify → Unleashed auto-updates
    • Fulfill in Unleashed: Complete the Sales Order in Unleashed → Optionally sync back to Shopify

    Recommendation: Fulfill in Shopify first, then Unleashed auto-completes when the order status changes.

    Sales Groups for Channel Analytics

    All Shopify orders are tagged with Sales Group: Shopify. This allows you to filter reports and dashboards by channel. You can also create sales groups for other channels:

    • Shopify – E-commerce orders
    • API/POS – Physical store orders imported via script
    • Wholesale – B2B orders

    Use these groupings to track revenue, margin, and inventory by channel.

    Troubleshooting Shopify Sync

    Commandes Not Syncing?

    Common issues and solutions:

    • Missing tax rates: Check Settings → Taxes. Add 20% and 5.5% rates if missing.
    • SKU mismatch: Verify Shopify SKU matches Unleashed product code. Use batch change to sync.
    • Connection error: Menu → Integrations → Shopify → Check Connection Log for errors.
    • Produits not created: Ensure "Create Missing Produits" is enabled in Shopify config.

    Importing Missing Commandes Manually

    If orders don't auto-import, use the manual import feature:

    1. Go to Integrations → Shopify
    2. Click Import Missing Commandes de vente
    3. Set the date range (e.g., "Jan 1 to Today")
    4. Click Import
    5. Check the Connection Log for any errors
    ✓
    Conseil : After fixing tax rates or SKU issues, always test with a recent Shopify order to confirm the sync works before processing hundreds of historical orders.

    Commandes d'achat

    ▼

    Purchase orders (POs) allow us to order stock from our suppliers. Properly managing POs ensures timely stock arrivals and accurate inventory levels.

    PO Status Lifecycle

    Purchase Order Statuses

    Every PO moves through these stages:

    StatusMeaning
    ParkedDraft — can be edited freely. No commitment to supplier yet.
    PlacedSent/committed — supplier expects delivery. Cannot change lines.
    Custom StatusesExtensions of Placed (e.g., "Confirmed", "In Transit") for tracking.
    ReceiptedStock received into inventory. You can still add cost lines.
    CompletedFully closed — no further changes possible.
    DeletedCancelled/removed from the system.

    Création d'un bon de commande

    Step-by-Step PO Creation

    1. Go to Purchases → Add Purchase (never create from within a Sales Order!)
    2. Select the Supplier (check they exist first — do not create duplicates)
    3. Set the warehouse to DEPOT_FEEL for all new stock
    4. Add products using FMSK_ code to search quickly
    5. Enter quantities and unit prices
    6. Verify tax rate: French products use 20% VAT, some skincare items use 5.5% reduced rate
    7. Add any additional costs (freight, duties) in the Cost Lines section
    8. Cost Distribution: Always use "By Base Quantity" (not "By Value")
    9. Review total and Save → Status = Parked
    10. When ready to send to supplier: change status to Placed
    Create purchase order

    Viewing Commandes d'achat

    Finding and Filtering POs

    To view existing purchase orders:

    1. Go to Purchases → View Purchases (or use the PO Kanban view)
    2. Filter by: Status, Entrepôt, Supplier, Date Range
    3. Use the Supplier Reference field to track supplier invoice numbers
    Purchase orders list with filters

    Receipting a Purchase Order

    Full Receipting Process

    When goods arrive from a supplier, receipt the PO to add stock to inventory:

    1. Open the PO and click Receipt
    2. Enter the Supplier Reference (invoice number) — this exports to Odoo for reconciliation
    3. Enter the Supplier Invoice Date
    4. Confirm receipt quantity per line (verify against the packing slip)
    5. Choose: Confirm Receipt (full delivery) or Split Receipt (partial delivery)

    After receipting: the Average Landed Cost (ALC) recalculates automatically, Last Cost updates, and the PO exports to Odoo.

    ⚠
    Important : You cannot set a historical receipt date — products become Stock on Hand from the date you process the receipt. Receipt promptly when goods arrive.

    Split Receipting (Partial Deliveries)

    When Only Part of the Order Arrives

    If a supplier ships only part of your order, use Split Receipt:

    1. Open the PO and click Receipt
    2. Enter the actual quantities received (less than ordered)
    3. Click Split Receipt

    What happens:

    • Receipted quantities go to Stock on Hand immediately
    • Remaining quantities create a new PO with a suffix (e.g., PO-00001936 → PO1-00001936/1)
    • You can still add costs to the receipted portion before completing

    Supplier Lead Times on POs

    Expected Delivery Date

    When you create a PO, Unleashed calculates the expected delivery date automatically:

    • If the supplier has a lead time set (e.g., 10 days), the delivery date is: Order Date + 10 days
    • If no lead time is set, the delivery date defaults to the order date (you must fill it in manually)

    This helps you plan stock availability and forecast when POs will arrive.

    Importing Missing Commandes from Fournisseurs

    Backfilling Historical POs

    If you need to import past orders (e.g., orders from Shopify that triggered POs in Unleashed):

    1. Go to Integrations (if applicable) or manually create POs
    2. Use Import/Export → Commandes d'achat for bulk import
    3. Create a CSV with: Supplier Code, Product Code, Quantity, Unit Price, Entrepôt
    4. Upload and confirm
    🔴
    CRITICAL: Back-Order Bug Warning
    DO NOT create a Purchase Order from within a Sales Order. There is a known bug: if you click "Create PO" on a Sales Order, it creates a linked PO that can cause the stock to be double-counted (once in the SO, once in the PO), resulting in inflated inventory. Always create POs from Purchases → Add Purchase.
    🔴
    CRITICAL: Never Receipt to Wrong Entrepôt
    Once receipted, the warehouse cannot be changed. Always verify the warehouse is set to DEPOT_FEEL before confirming receipt.
    ⚠
    Important : Do not complete a PO until all costs have been added. You cannot add freight, duties, or other costs after completing.
    ✓
    Conseil : Always enter the Supplier Reference (invoice number) before receipting — this is essential for Odoo reconciliation downstream.

    3. Select the Entrepôt for shipping
    4. Add product lines with quantities

    Step 3: Sales Groups for Analytics

    Always assign a Sales Group to each order. This enables reporting by channel:

    • Shopify – E-commerce orders (auto-assigned for Shopify orders)
    • API/POS – Physical store orders from POS script
    • Wholesale – B2B wholesale orders

    These groups are used in dashboards and reports to analyze revenue, margin, and inventory by channel.

    Step 4: Prepare and Fulfill the Order

    When the order is ready to ship:

    1. In the SO, click Dispatch or Ship (depending on Unleashed version)

    Shopify Order Status Mapping

    Shopify orders appear in Unleashed with these statuses:

    • Parked/Placed: Order received from Shopify, awaiting fulfillment
    • Backordered: Partial shipment sent, balance awaiting stock (rare – avoid manually creating POs from SO)
    • Shipped/Completed: Order fulfilled and marked complete in Unleashed

    Difference Between Fulfilling in Shopify vs Unleashed

    Best Practice: Fulfill in Shopify First

    The recommended workflow is:

    1. In Shopify: Pack the order, print the label, mark items as "Fulfilled" in Shopify
    2. In Unleashed: The order status automatically updates (usually to Shipped/Completed)
    3. Stock is automatically deducted from inventory when Shopify sync processes the fulfillment
    ⚠
    Important : Always verify that stock is available before creating a SO. If stock is insufficient, the order may be set to "Backordered", which complicates month-end closing. Check the stock indicator (green = available, red = short) before confirming the order.

    Ajustements des stocks

    ▼

    Unleashed has two tools for correcting inventory: Stock Count and Stock Ajustement. Understanding the difference is critical.

    Stock Count vs Stock Ajustement

    Stock Count (Full Stocks Reset)

    Use Stock Count when you need to reset and re-enter all stock from zero for a warehouse — for example, when opening a new location or doing a full physical inventory:

    1. Go to Inventory → Stock Count → Add Count
    2. Name the count (e.g., "Stocks March 2026")
    3. Select the warehouse
    4. Add products manually, via barcode scanning, or via Import (upload an Excel template)
    5. Enter the actual quantities for each product
    6. Save and complete — the system replaces old quantities with your new counts
    ✓
    Conseil : To get a pre-filled template, go to Reports → Stock on Hand, filter by warehouse, and export to XLS. This gives you all product codes with current quantities — edit the quantities column and re-import into Stock Count.

    Stock Ajustement (Individual Corrections)

    Use Stock Ajustement when you need to correct specific products without resetting everything — for example, expired items, damaged goods, or returns from boutiques:

    1. Go to Inventory → Stock Adjustments → Add Ajustement
    2. Select the Entrepôt (very important — choose the right one first!)
    3. Add the product(s) you need to adjust
    4. Enter the quantity: positive = add stock, negative = remove stock
    5. Select a reason and add notes
    6. Save

    Example: 5 creams expired at DEPOT_FEEL → select DEPOT_FEEL, find the product, enter -5, reason: "Damage".

    Stock adjustments
    Stock adjustments

    Step 2: Create a New Ajustement

    Click + New Ajustement:

    1. Select the Entrepôt (e.g., DEPOT_FEEL, CLICHY)
    2. Add adjustment lines:
      • Select the Product
      • Enter the Quantity (positive = add stock, negative = remove stock)
      • Select a Reason (see Common Ajustement Reasons below)
    3. Add notes if needed (e.g., "Found broken serum bottles in Clichy store")
    4. Click Save

    Common Ajustement Reasons

    • Stocks Count: Discrepancy found during a physical count
    • Damage: Damaged products discovered (broken bottles, expired, etc.)
    • Shrinkage: Unexplained loss (theft, evaporation, etc.)
    • Stock Transfert: Manual transfer between warehouses (prefer using Transferts entre entrepôts instead)
    • Sample: Taken for demonstration or staff testing
    • Return: Customer return not recorded through the SO system
    • Recount: Correction after a recount confirmed the discrepancy

    Month-End Ajustements des stocks

    Handling Stock Shortfalls at Month-End

    When closing a month in Unleashed, you may find that stock levels don't match orders. This is common with POS orders and physical stores. Solution:

    1. Try to bulk-complete all SOs for the month
    2. If the system shows "insufficient stock", note which products are short
    3. Create a stock adjustment with reason "Stocks Count" for each shortfall
    4. Re-try the bulk completion

    Example: "API channel sold 5 units of SERUM-001 but Unleashed only had 3 on hand. Create adjustment: +2 units, reason: Stocks Count"

    ⚠
    Avertissement : Stock adjustments directly affect inventory levels and reports. Always record a clear reason for traceability. If you adjust stock incorrectly, it skews reports and COGS calculations.

    Transferts entre entrepôts

    ▼

    Creating a Entrepôt Transfert

    Step 1: Access Transferts

    Menu → Stock → Transferts entre entrepôts.

    Entrepôt transfers

    Step 2: Create a New Transfert

    Click + New Transfert:

    1. Select the Source Entrepôt (e.g., DEPOT_FEEL)
    2. Select the Destination Entrepôt (e.g., CLICHY store)
    3. Add products — you can type the FMSK_ code, or use barcode scanning:
      • Click the barcode scan button
      • Scan all products (multi-scan supported), then click Add
      • Enter quantities manually for each scanned product
    4. Click Save

    Step 3: Confirm Receipt

    Once the stock has been physically transferred and received, click Receive to finalize the transfer:

    1. Verify the quantities match what was received
    2. Note any discrepancies (damaged in transit, etc.)
    3. Click Confirm

    Stock is then debited from the source warehouse and credited to the destination.

    Simplified Entrepôt Strategy

    Shopify Default Entrepôt → Depot Fill Strategy

    To avoid constant stock transfers, Feel My Skin uses this simplified approach:

    • Shopify orders: All route to the "Shopify" default warehouse (set in Shopify Hub config)
    • Depot Fill transfers: Periodically move stock from DEPOT_FEEL to the Shopify warehouse in one bulk transfer
    • Physical stores: Stock is held at each store location and managed via POS/API orders

    This reduces the number of transfers and makes inventory easier to track.

    ✓
    Conseil : Schedule transfers regularly (e.g., weekly or bi-weekly) to balance stock between DEPOT_FEEL, the three Paris stores, and the Romanian warehouses. Use the Reorder Report to see low stock locations and plan transfers.

    Rapports et tableaux de bord

    Unleashed provides powerful reporting and custom dashboards to analyze inventory, profitability, and sales by channel, product, and warehouse.

    Standard Rapports

    Stock on Hand Inquiry

    View current stock levels across all warehouses. You can also export to XLS to get a spreadsheet of all products and quantities for a specific warehouse — useful for inventory counts and stock count imports.

    1. Menu → Rapports → Stock on Hand Inquiry
    2. Filter by:
      • Entrepôt (DEPOT_FEEL, CLICHY, GAITE, etc.)
      • Product Code or Group
      • Status (Active, Obsolete)
    3. Run the report to see:
      • Units on hand (physical stock)
      • Committed stock (allocated to SOs)
      • Available stock (on hand minus committed)
      • Unit cost and total value

    Product Margin Inquiry

    Analyze profitability by product and channel:

    1. Menu → Rapports → Product Margin Inquiry
    2. Group by Product Code or Product Group
    3. Filter by:
      • Date range (e.g., "Jan 1 to Jan 31" for a month)
      • Sales Group (Shopify, API, Wholesale) for channel comparison
    4. The report shows:
      • Revenue (gross sales)
      • COGS (cost of goods sold)
      • Gross Profit (Revenue - COGS)
      • Gross Margin % (Profit / Revenue)

    Use this to identify high-margin vs low-margin products and optimize pricing strategy.

    Costing Analysis Report

    Deep dive into inventory costs and valuation:

    1. Menu → Rapports → Costing Analysis Report
    2. Shows:
      • Average landed cost per unit (ALC)
      • Total inventory value
      • COGS per transaction
      • Cost allocation method verification
    3. Use this to identify COGS issues (e.g., inflated COGS due to wrong cost distribution method)

    Custom Dashboards

    Build dashboards tailored to your role:

    1. Menu → Dashboards
    2. Click + New Dashboard or Edit Dashboard
    3. Choose widgets to add (Chart, Grid, KPI, Table, etc.)
    4. For each widget:
      • Select the data source (Sales, Stocks, Purchases, etc.)
      • Choose the metric (Revenue, Gross Profit, Stock Count, etc.)
      • Group by warehouse, product, channel, date, etc.
      • Set time range (daily, weekly, monthly)

    2. Click on any bar (e.g., "Shopify") to drill down and see products within that channel
    3. Click again on a specific product to see all details for that product in that channel

    Clôture de fin de mois

    ▼

    Month-end close is a critical process. It finalizes all transactions, ensures inventory accuracy, and closes the accounting period so reports reflect final numbers.

    Step-by-Step Month-End Process

    Step 1: Complete All Open Commandes de vente (Bulk)

    In the last days of the month, complete all SOs:

    1. Menu → Commandes de vente
    2. Filter by Status: Open or Placed
    3. Select ALL orders (use the checkbox at the top to select all on page, repeat for all pages)
    4. Click Bulk Actions → Complete
    5. Click Process

    Expected Outcome: System processes all orders and updates stock. Some may succeed, some may fail if stock is insufficient.

    Step 2: Handle Failed Commandes (Ajustements des stocks)

    For orders that failed to complete due to insufficient stock:

    1. Identify which products are short (system will list them)
    2. For each shortfall, create a Stock Ajustement:
      • Go to Stock → Ajustements des stocks
      • Create an adjustment with Reason: Stocks Count
      • Add the missing units (e.g., if SERUM-001 needs 3 more units, adjust +3)
      • Save
    3. Re-run the bulk complete for the failed orders

    Repeat until all orders are completed successfully.

    Step 3: Verify Stock Reconciliation

    After completing all orders, verify that physical stock matches Unleashed:

    1. Run Stock on Hand Inquiry for each warehouse
    2. Compare Unleashed numbers to your physical count (if available)
    3. If discrepancies exist, create adjustments with reason "Recount" to correct them

    Step 4: Close the Period (Accounting)

    Once all orders are completed and stock is reconciled, close the period:

    1. Menu → Settings → Period Management (or similar, depending on your Unleashed version)
    2. Select the month/period to close (e.g., "January 2026")
    3. Click Close Period
    4. Confirm that you want to finalize this period (typically can't edit transactions after closing)

    Step 5: Generate Final Rapports

    Once the period is closed, run month-end reports for accounting/finance:

    • Product Margin Inquiry – Gross profit by product and channel
    • Costing Analysis Report – Total COGS and inventory value
    • Stock on Hand Inquiry – Final inventory levels by warehouse
    • Export to CSV and provide to your finance/accounting team

    Common Month-End Issues & Solutions

    Issue: "Insufficient Stock" Errors When Bulk Completing Commandes

    Cause: POS orders and physical store orders have imprecise stock tracking. The system shows fewer units on hand than actually sold.

    Solution: Create stock adjustments to correct the difference and re-run the bulk complete.

    Issue: COGS Appears Inflated

    Cause: Cost distribution method is set to "by value" instead of "by base quantity".

    Solution: Check each product's cost distribution method. Change to "by base quantity" and recalculate.

    Issue: Stock Discrepancies Persist

    Cause: Missing orders, incorrect POS imports, or duplicate stock transfers.

    Solution: Use Transaction Inquiry to trace stock movements and identify the source of the discrepancy.

    ⚠
    Best Practice: Plan for 2-3 hours for month-end close, especially in the first few months. The process will become faster once you understand your data patterns and stock accuracy improves.
    ✓
    Conseil : After closing a period, create a backup or snapshot of the data. This protects against accidental changes and provides an audit trail.

    • Purchase orders awaiting receipt (check expected arrival dates)

    Check Commandes d'achat in "Placed" status. If goods have arrived:

    2. Click Receive on the PO

    3. Confirm no orders are stuck in "Parked" or "Placed" status unexpectedly
    End of day checks

    Règles critiques

    ▼

    These rules must always be followed to avoid costly mistakes and maintain data integrity.

    🔴
    Rule 1: Cost Distribution = "By Base Quantity" (NOT "By Value")
    Every product MUST have cost distribution set to "by base quantity". Using "by value" inflates COGS and distorts margin analysis. Check this setting regularly, especially for imported products.
    🔴
    Rule 2: NEVER Remove Tax Rate 0%
    Keep the 0% tax rate in Settings → Taxes. It's essential for POS/wholesale orders which don't carry tax. Adding 20% and 5.5% for Shopify is fine, but NEVER delete the 0% default.
    🔴
    Rule 3: Never Create PO from Sales Order (Back-Order Bug)
    DO NOT click "Create PO" within a Sales Order. This creates a linked PO that doubles inventory counts. Create POs separately in the PO menu. If you accidentally do this, use Transaction Inquiry to delete the duplicate.
    🔴
    Rule 4: Check Stock Before Selling
    Before creating an SO (or accepting a Shopify order), verify stock is available in the correct warehouse. Unavailable stock creates backlogs and complicates fulfillment.
    🔴
    Rule 5: Batch Change Product SKUs, Don't Change One by One
    If syncing Shopify SKUs into Unleashed, use the Batch Change feature (Produits → Change Codes → Batch). This is faster and prevents individual errors.
    🔴
    Rule 6: Record Reasons for All Ajustements
    Every stock adjustment (Damage, Shrinkage, Stocks Count, etc.) must have a documented reason. This ensures traceability and audit compliance.
    🔴
    Rule 7: Confirm Receipts Promptly
    As soon as supplier stock is received, click "Receive" in the PO. This updates stock levels in real time and prevents inventory discrepancies.
    🔴
    Rule 8: Use Correct Entrepôt Codes
    Always use the correct warehouse (DEPOT_FEEL, CLICHY, GAITE, CHATELET, etc.). A wrong selection creates discrepancies and breaks reporting.
    🔴
    Rule 9: Never Delete an Order
    If an order is incorrect, mark it as "Cancelled" or create a note. Never delete it, as this loses history and makes auditing impossible.
    🔴
    Rule 10: Check Prices Regularly
    Purchase and selling prices must be kept up to date. Outdated prices lead to incorrect margins and inaccurate POs.
    🔴
    Rule 11: Sync Shopify Tax Rates Before Importing Commandes
    Create 20% and 5.5% tax rates in Settings → Taxes BEFORE importing Shopify orders. Without matching tax rates, orders fail to import.
    🔴
    Rule 12: Never Validate Without Confirming First
    When learning the system, practice without clicking Complete or Validate. It is always better not to validate than to validate and discover you cannot undo it. If you are unsure, ask your manager before confirming any transaction — POs, stock adjustments, transfers, and sales orders. You can save as Parked/Draft and come back to it.
    🔴
    Rule 13: When in Doubt, Contact a Manager
    If something seems abnormal (negative stock, inconsistent price, stuck transfer, sync errors), do not try to fix it alone. Report it immediately to your manager or the Unleashed implementation team.

    Common Mistakes & How to Fix Them

    ▼

    Mistakes happen — what matters is knowing how to correct them. Completed transactions in Unleashed cannot be deleted or edited, so you must use corrective transactions instead.

    Receipted the Wrong Product or Quantity

    1. Create a Supplier Return to reverse the incorrect receipt
    2. The stock will be deducted back from inventory
    3. Create a corrected purchase order with the right products/quantities

    Receipted to the Wrong Entrepôt

    You cannot change the warehouse after receipt. Instead:

    1. Create a Entrepôt Transfert from the incorrect warehouse to the correct one
    2. Process and confirm the transfer

    Completed a PO with Errors

    Once a PO is completed, it cannot be reopened. Instead:

    1. Create a Supplier Return to reverse the transaction
    2. Create a new, corrected purchase order

    Stock Discrepancy (Physical vs. System)

    If your physical inventory count doesn't match what Unleashed shows:

    1. Perform a Stock Count to formally reconcile
    2. Create Ajustements des stocks to correct the variance
    3. Always record a reason (Stocks Count, Damage, Shrinkage, etc.)

    Accidentally Created PO from Sales Order (Back-Order Bug)

    1. Go to Reports → Transaction Inquiry
    2. Find the duplicate stock movement created by the linked PO
    3. Delete or reverse the duplicate entry
    4. Create a proper PO from Purchases → Add Purchase if still needed
    🔴
    Remember: Completed transactions in Unleashed CANNOT be deleted or edited. You must always use corrective transactions (Supplier Returns, Ajustements des stocks, Transferts entre entrepôts) to fix mistakes.
