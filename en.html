Introduction - About Unleashed

▼

Unleashed is an Inventory Management System used by Feel My Skin to:

  • Track inventory levels across 7 warehouses in France and Romania
  • Manage purchase orders from our suppliers
  • Process customer orders for B2C and B2B sales
  • Record stock movements: transfers, adjustments, assemblies
  • Generate reports for analysis and planning
✓
Tip: Unleashed is accessible at https://live.unleashedsoftware.com. Use your FMS credentials to log in.

Product Management

▼

Products in Unleashed include all items sold by Feel My Skin: serums, creams, sets, etc.

Step 1: Access Product Management

From the main menu, click Products. You will see the full list of all active products.

Products list

Step 2: View Product Details

Click on the product name to view its details: SKU code, purchase price, selling price, description, images, suppliers.

Product details
⚠
Warning: Only modify the SKU or product code if you are certain. This can affect existing orders.

Step 3: Add a New Product

Click + New Product. Fill in the required fields: name, SKU, purchase price, selling price, default supplier.

Key Information for Each Product

  • SKU (Stock Keeping Unit): Unique code (e.g., SERUM-001)
  • Barcode: Barcode for quick scanning
  • Unit of Measure: Unit (piece, box, carton)
  • Cost Price: Purchase price from the supplier
  • Retail Price: Customer selling price
  • Supplier: Default supplier
  • Reorder Level: Minimum stock before reordering

Purchase Orders

▼

Purchase orders (POs) allow us to order stock from our suppliers.

Step 1: Access Purchase Orders

Menu → Purchase Orders to view all POs. You can filter by status: Open, Placed, Received, Completed.

Purchase orders list

Step 2: Create a New PO

Click on + New Purchase Order:

  1. Select the Supplier
  2. Select the destination Warehouse
  3. Add product lines
  4. Enter quantities and prices
  5. Check the total
  6. Click Save
Create purchase order

Step 3: Place the Order

Once saved, click Place Order to send the PO to the supplier. The status changes to "Placed".

Step 4: Receive Stock

When the order arrives, click Receive. Unleashed then creates a receipt to record incoming stock.

✓
Tip: Always verify received quantities against ordered quantities. If there is a discrepancy, note it in the receipt.

Sales Orders

▼

Sales orders (SOs) record sales to B2C and B2B customers.

Step 1: Access Sales Orders

Menu → Sales Orders to view all SOs. Filter by status: Open, Completed, Backordered.

Sales orders list

Step 2: Create a New SO

Click on + New Sales Order:

  1. Select the Customer
  2. Select the Warehouse for shipping
  3. Add product lines
  4. Check stock availability
  5. Set the desired delivery date
  6. Click Save
Create sales order

Step 3: Prepare and Ship the Order

When the order is ready, click Dispatch or Ship. Unleashed records the stock departure and generates a shipping document.

⚠
Important: Always verify that stock is available before creating a SO. If stock is insufficient, the order will go to "Backordered".

Stock Adjustments

▼

Adjustments allow you to correct counting errors, record breakages, or stock losses.

Step 1: Access Adjustments

Menu → Stock → Stock Adjustments to view all adjustments.

Stock adjustments

Step 2: Create a New Adjustment

Click + New Adjustment:

  1. Select the Warehouse
  2. Select the Product
  3. Enter the adjusted quantity (positive = add, negative = remove)
  4. Enter a Reason: Damage, Shrinkage, Inventory Count, etc.
  5. Click Save
⚠
Warning: Stock adjustments directly affect inventory levels and reports. Always record a clear reason for traceability.

Common Adjustment Reasons

  • Inventory Count: Discrepancy found during a physical count
  • Damage: Damaged products discovered
  • Shrinkage: Unexplained loss / assessed losses
  • Stock Transfer: Manual transfer between warehouses
  • Sample: Taken for demonstration/testing
  • Return: Customer return not recorded as such

Warehouse Transfers

▼

Transfers allow you to move stock from one warehouse to another (e.g., from the depot to a store).

Step 1: Access Transfers

Menu → Stock → Warehouse Transfers.

Warehouse transfers

Step 2: Create a New Transfer

Click + New Transfer:

  1. Select the Source Warehouse
  2. Select the Destination Warehouse
  3. Add product lines
  4. Enter quantities to transfer
  5. Click Save
Create transfer

Step 3: Confirm Receipt

Once the stock has been physically transferred and received, click Receive to finalize the transfer. Stock is then debited from the source warehouse and credited to the destination.

✓
Tip: Schedule transfers regularly to balance stock between DEPOT_FEEL, the three Paris stores, and the Romanian warehouses.

Daily Checks

▼

Each day, you should perform certain checks to maintain the health of the Unleashed system.

Morning: Check Pending Orders

Open the Dashboard and look for:

  • Sales orders in "Open" status ready to be shipped
  • Purchase orders awaiting receipt
  • Low stock (Stock on-hand near the Reorder Level)
Daily dashboard view

Midday/Afternoon: Confirm Receipts

Check Purchase Orders in "Placed" status. If goods have arrived, click Receive and record them.

End of Day: Close Orders

Mark shipped orders as Completed. This updates the statistics and clears the pending tasks list.

End of day checks

Weekly: Stock Report

Every Friday, generate a Stock Report to see the level of each product per warehouse. Identify low or excess stock.

Key Checkpoints

  • ✓ No orders in "Backorder" status for more than 3 days
  • ✓ Minimum stock (Reorder Level) reached → Create a PO
  • ✓ All warehouse transfers confirmed (Received)
  • ✓ No discrepancies between Unleashed and physical count
  • ✓ Selling and purchase prices up to date in the product record

Critical Rules

▼

These rules must always be followed to avoid costly mistakes.

🔴
Rule 1: Check stock before selling
Before creating a SO (Sales Order), make sure stock is available in the correct warehouse. Unavailable stock creates backlogs.
🔴
Rule 2: Never modify a product SKU
SKUs must remain constant. Changing them affects all history. If a correction is needed, create a new product instead.
🔴
Rule 3: Record reasons for all adjustments
Every stock adjustment (Damage, Shrinkage, etc.) must have a documented reason. This ensures traceability.
🔴
Rule 4: Confirm receipts promptly
As soon as supplier stock is received, click "Receive" in the PO. This updates stock levels in real time.
🔴
Rule 5: Use the correct warehouses
Always use the correct warehouse code (CLICHY, GAITE, CHATLET, DEPOT_FEEL, etc.). A wrong selection creates discrepancies.
🔴
Rule 6: Never delete an order
If an order is incorrect, mark it as "Cancelled" or create a note. Never delete it, as this loses the history.
🔴
Rule 7: Check prices regularly
Purchase and selling prices must be kept up to date. Outdated prices lead to incorrect margins.
🔴
Rule 8: When in doubt, contact a manager
If something seems abnormal (negative stock, inconsistent price, stuck transfer), do not try to fix it alone. Report it immediately.
Summary of critical rules

Troubleshooting

Problem Solution
Negative stock (-1, -5, etc.) Create an Adjustment to correct it. Check the movement history.
Order stuck / Cannot save Check that all required fields are filled in. Contact support if the error persists.
Transfer not received / Stuck in transit Go to Warehouse Transfers, find the transfer, click "Receive" to finalize it.
Stock differs from physical count Create an adjustment with reason "Inventory Count" and record the difference.
Supplier not found Check the spelling. If missing, create a new supplier via Products → Suppliers.
